GRWRHITECH ₹191 Cr Capex Record FY26 120% Dividend 6 May 2026

Garware Hi-Tech Films delivers record FY26, doubles down with ₹191 Cr Sun Control Film line at Waluj

Garware Hi-Tech Films posted its best-ever annual numbers and immediately committed to brownfield expansion. FY26 consolidated revenue ₹2,120 Cr, with full-year PAT ₹338 Cr. Q4 alone delivered record momentum: revenue ₹597 Cr (+30.1% QoQ, +8.9% YoY) — the highest quarterly profitability in the company's 90-year history. Board recommended a final dividend ₹12/share (120% of face value).

On the same day, the Board approved a new Sun Control Film (SCF) lamination line at Waluj, Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar — total investment ₹191 Cr plus taxes, adding 1,200 LSF/yr on top of existing 4,200 LSF/yr base (already at optimum utilisation). Funded entirely through internal accruals; commissioning in 14 months. New facility incorporates advanced robotics and automation.

Management's FY27 guidance: revenue growth 15–20%, EBITDA margin 22–25%, exports >70% of revenue, Value-Added Products >85%. Notably, the company has delivered this record year while absorbing a 50% US tariff structure on key export products — Q3 FY26 revenue had declined only 1.6% YoY despite tariffs, and Q4 reversed the trend entirely. Monika Garware re-appointed Vice-Chairperson & Joint MD for 5 years (Nov 2026 – Oct 2031).

Why it matters: A specialty-films business turning record numbers in spite of crippling tariffs is unusual. The 1,200 LSF capex (≈29% capacity addition) signals management sees consumer-direct demand pull-through from the Garware Application Studio and Garware Home Solutions retail expansion holding up — and isn't waiting for tariff relief to commit.

KIRLPNU 100% Acquisition CRISIL Upgrade 4–5 May 2026

Kirloskar Pneumatic takes Systems & Components India to 100% for ₹12.55 Cr; CRISIL upgrades to AA-Stable

On 4 May, KPCL completed acquisition of the remaining 44.74% stake in Systems & Components India Private Limited from the existing promoter shareholder for ₹12.55 Cr, taking ownership to 100%. The subsidiary now becomes wholly-owned, effective immediately.

Next day, CRISIL upgraded the company's long-term rating from CRISIL AA-/Positive → CRISIL AA/Stable, with short-term rating reaffirmed at A1+. The rating covers an enhanced facility limit of ₹840 Cr (from ₹636 Cr) — both rating and limit increases reflect sustained operational improvement and a robust financial risk profile.

Context: KPCL closed FY26 (per 30 Apr call) with Revenue ₹706 Cr for Q4 and FY26 net profit ₹258 Cr; final dividend ₹8.50/share recommended; stock split approved. Market leader in CNG systems (60%+ share) and ammonia refrigeration (70%+ share). Market cap ~₹10,355 Cr; almost debt-free.

ENTERO Subsidiary Merger 6 May 2026

Entero Healthcare gets MCA fast-track nod to merge Chethana & CPD Pharma into Rada Medisolutions

MCA Southern Region (Chennai) approved on 17 Apr 2026 (order received 5 May, disclosed 6 May) the fast-track amalgamation under Section 233 — folding two wholly-owned subsidiaries (Chethana Pharma Distributors and CPD Pharma) into another subsidiary Rada Medisolutions. Effective from appointed date 15 Apr 2025. No new shares issued since all are wholly-owned; transferor entity capital cancelled. Authorised capital of Rada to be enhanced to ₹1.35 Cr post-merger.

Rationale: operational efficiencies, scale economies, reduction in overhead and compliance costs, simplification of group structure. All three companies were in pharmaceutical/surgical distribution. Recent operating trajectory remains strong — Q2 FY26 PAT ₹31.6 Cr (+33.78% YoY) on revenue ₹1,571 Cr (+20.78%). Jefferies has the stock at Buy with target ₹1,320. Market cap ₹5,507 Cr; P/E 58.

RAINBOW GST Appeal 8 May 2026

Rainbow Children's Medicare GST demand appeal disallowed; will pursue further legal recourse

Rainbow disclosed that the appeal it had filed against a GST demand notice under Section 74(5) CGST Act (issued by DGGI Bengaluru Zonal Unit) was disallowed by the Principal Commissioner of Customs & Central Tax (Appeals-1), with order received by post on 7 May. The company is now preparing a further appeal before the appropriate appellate authority on subject-matter-expert advice. No payment/compensation/penalty made yet, and management states no material impact on financial position or operations at this stage. Action does not involve KMP, promoter, or ultimate person in control.

Why it matters: Healthcare GST exposure (input-credit eligibility on inpatient care, room-rent thresholds) has been a recurring industry-wide issue. Worth tracking the next forum's view; if the matter goes to the GST Appellate Tribunal, timelines could stretch 12-24 months.
