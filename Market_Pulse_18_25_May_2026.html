MARINE₹208.72 Cr Order18 May 2026

Marine Electricals bags ₹208.72 Cr twin order from Adani Infra & Udupi Cochin Shipyard — ~25% of annual revenue in a single day

Marine Electricals (India) — one of India's largest LV/MV switchgear makers, Tier-1 electrical supplier to the Indian Navy and global shipyards — announced orders worth ₹208.72 Cr (ex-taxes) on 18 May. Two contracts: a power distribution system for Adani Infra (12-14 month delivery) and an Alarm Monitoring System for Udupi Cochin Shipyard (24 month delivery). No related-party involvement.

Scale context: against FY-trailing revenue of ~₹850 Cr, this single-day award is roughly a quarter of annual revenue and meaningfully lifts order-book visibility. The company has a 50% share in its electrical segment, 68.2% promoter holding, and has compounded profit at 45.9% CAGR over 5 years. Recent order momentum has been strong (₹284 Cr in data-centre/marine in earlier weeks, ₹70.86 Cr from Princeton Digital + C-Torq in late April). Watch item: high debtor days (~157) mean working-capital discipline as the book scales is the key execution risk.

Why it matters: the Adani Infra power-distribution award signals data-centre / infrastructure electrical demand, while the Cochin Shipyard alarm-monitoring order reinforces the defence-shipbuilding moat. Two structurally different demand drivers in one filing.
