Lead Story — Defence Contract Continuation

1 Card
HFCL₹135 Cr RailTel AMCStock +9% · 52-wk High27 May 2026

HFCL wins ₹135.09 Cr RailTel 5-year AMC for defence Secure Operations Network — third consecutive week of headline action

HFCL bagged a ₹135.09 Cr Annual Maintenance Contract from RailTel Corporation on 27 May — a 5-year AMC running till January 2031 covering the previously-executed "Secure Operations (OPS) Network for Data Centres of Indian Defence Forces". Scope: end-to-end maintenance of one central data centre + 120 mini data centres, including hardware/software/data-centre infrastructure with AI-enabled network security, plus 24×7 monitoring, preventive & corrective maintenance, incident management, and performance optimisation. Renewal-style award — RailTel chose HFCL post-warranty rather than running fresh tender, an implicit performance endorsement on a defence-grade project.

Stock reacted sharply — +8-9% intraday to ₹176.44 (52-week high) on 27 May. The shares have rallied roughly 150% in two months, riding momentum from the March $1.1bn / ₹10,159 Cr global OFC mega-order, the ₹230 Cr defence-facility approval covered earlier this month, and the warrant-led ₹555 Cr promoter commitment. At ~₹27 Cr per year on annualised basis the AMC isn't a financial needle-mover (against ~₹4,900 Cr revenue base), but the quality of revenue — recurring, mission-critical, defence-anchored — is the read-through. Third consecutive Market Pulse with HFCL in the headline area.

Why it matters: Successful AMC retention after warranty is a strong signal that the original Secure OPS implementation met defence-grade quality benchmarks — a moat against new entrants for follow-on defence networking contracts.

Sources: NSE — RailTel PO Order Detail Business Standard
