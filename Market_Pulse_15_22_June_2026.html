HCGCDSCO 24-Month SuspensionClinical Trial Violations17 June 2026

CDSCO suspends HCG's KR Unit Ethics Committee for 24 months — failure to report SAEs, casualties, conflict of interest

HCG disclosed on 17 June that the Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation (CDSCO) issued an order dated 12 June 2026 suspending the registration of the HCG Bangalore Institute of Oncology — KR Road Unit Ethics Committee from approving or overseeing any new clinical trial or BA/BE study for a period of 24 months. Order issued by DCGI Dr Rajeev Singh Raghuvanshi under Rule 14.3(iv) of the New Drugs and Clinical Trials Rules, 2019.

CDSCO's findings: the EC failed to report at least three cases each of injury and death to CDSCO within prescribed timelines, did not provide details on four cases each of injuries and deaths, plus documentation lapses and deficiencies in conflict-of-interest controls and patient safety oversight. Order follows two risk-based inspections at HCG Bangalore in July 2025 — a chain of events that traces back to the resignation of then-EC Chairperson Justice P Krishna Bhat (Retd) in June 2025, after the institute failed to act on his discrepancy findings during clinical trials. South First, the originating investigative source, has been publishing on this since June 2025.

During suspension, the EC has been directed to continue monitoring all ongoing trials, submit monthly safety reports, file CAPA reports and causality assessments for all previously-unreported SAEs, and provide compensation details where applicable. HCG's defensive framing: the suspension is "limited to this single ethics committee and does not affect other ethics committees, hospital operations, or patient care" and management expects no significant financial impact — currently reviewing the order and exploring legal remedies.

Stock reaction was contained — −1.06% to ₹609 by 19 June close. Operating context: HCG runs 2,600+ beds across 25 facilities in India and Kenya; KKR holds majority control (covered earlier in the May edition during the BACC/Milann divestment). Real impact will play out in the BA/BE-study revenue line (small but visible), oncology-trial relationships with pharma sponsors, and the reputational angle around clinical-research governance.
