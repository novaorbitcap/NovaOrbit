SHRIRAMFIN$4.4B FDI — Largest Ever in Indian FS8 Apr 2026

MUFG Bank Completes ₹3,962 Cr Investment — 20% Stake in Shriram Finance; Largest Cross-Border FDI in India's Financial Services

Japan's MUFG Bank (Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group) has completed its landmark investment in Shriram Finance, subscribing to 47.11 Cr equity shares at ₹840.93/share. Total investment: approximately ₹3,962 Cr (~$4.4B at announcement) for a 20% stake on fully-diluted basis. This is the largest cross-border investment in India's financial services sector ever. Shriram Finance — India's 2nd largest retail NBFC with AUM >₹2.91 trillion, 3,225 branches, 77,724 employees, and 9.7M customers — now has one of the world's largest banking groups as a strategic partner. Board approved allotment on 8 Apr. CCI approval already secured.

Sector Signal — Japan Inc Bets Big on India's Financial Stack

MUFG's $4.4B Shriram Finance investment is not just the largest cross-border financial services FDI in India — it signals Japan's deepening structural bet on India's financial inclusion story. Shriram's 9.7M customers (mostly small road transport operators and SMEs) represent a segment that global banks cannot reach directly. With MUFG bringing funding diversification, risk frameworks, and potential yen bond market access, Shriram gets a global capital advantage that few Indian NBFCs can match. Watch for SBI, HDFC, and Bajaj Finance to now face competitive pressure from a globally-backed Shriram.

5 updates
MULTI4 Companies — Same Drug7-8 Apr 2026

Dapagliflozin Race: Zydus, Biocon, Lupin & Aurobindo All Receive US FDA Approval — On the Same Day

In a remarkable regulatory event, four Indian pharma companies received US FDA final approvals for Dapagliflozin tablets (anti-diabetic, SGLT2 inhibitor, blockbuster drug) nearly simultaneously: Zydus Lifesciences (5mg & 10mg), Biocon Pharma (subsidiary), Lupin, and Aurobindo Pharma (Dapagliflozin + Metformin combo). This signals a massive wave of generic competition for AstraZeneca's Farxiga — a $6B+ global brand. Price erosion will be swift. For Indian pharma, this is revenue opportunity; for US healthcare, it's cost savings.

Sector Signal — Generic Dapagliflozin: India's Pharma Manufacturing Power on Display

Four Indian companies receiving FDA approval for the same blockbuster drug within days is a showcase of India's generics manufacturing depth. AstraZeneca's Farxiga generated ~$6.4B globally in 2024 — the generic opportunity is massive. With Zydus, Biocon, Lupin, and Aurobindo all launching simultaneously, US pricing will compress rapidly.
