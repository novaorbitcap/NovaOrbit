TCSQ4 FY26 Results9 Apr 2026

TCS Q4: Revenue ₹70,698 Cr (+9.6% YoY), Profit ₹13,718 Cr (+12% YoY), AI Revenue Crosses $2.3B — ₹31 Final Dividend

Q4 FY26: Revenue ₹70,698 Cr (+9.6% YoY, +5.4% QoQ, +1.2% QoQ CC). Net profit ₹13,718 Cr (+12% YoY, +29% QoQ). TCV: $12B in Q4 (3 mega deals). Operating margin 25% (ex one-offs) — highest in 4 years (+70 bps YoY). AI annualized revenue crossed $2.3B.
FY26 Full Year: Revenue ₹2,67,021 Cr (+4.6% YoY). TCV: $40.7B (5 mega deals). Total shareholder payout: ₹39,571 Cr. Final dividend ₹31/share (total FY26: ₹110/share). Client additions: $100M+ clients up to 66, $50M+ to 139. Cash flow 106.7% of net profit.
CEO K Krithivasan: "Third consecutive quarter of sequential growth...AI‑led positioning across services." CFO Samir Seksaria: "Margins expanded 70 bps even as we scaled AI investments."

Board Outcome · Press Release · BT Key Takeaways

Sector Signal — What TCS Q4 Tells Us About Indian IT

TCS's beat on every metric sets a constructive tone for IT results season. The $12B Q4 TCV (with 3 mega deals) signals deal pipelines are converting. AI revenue crossing $2.3B annualized is the most important number — it proves AI spending is moving from POCs to production at scale. Margin expansion (+70 bps) despite AI investments shows operating leverage is intact. Combined with Wipro's $1B+ Olam deal (Ed #5), Cyient's Kinetic Technologies acquisition (Ed #6), and LTIMindtree's MIT AI training (Ed #6), Indian IT is entering FY27 with the strongest positioning since the 2020 digital wave. Watch Infosys (expected ~Apr 17) for confirmation.
